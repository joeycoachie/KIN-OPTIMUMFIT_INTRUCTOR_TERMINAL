The Kinetic Six Select to Expand

Rule: Keep the hips steady and level before moving the legs.

  • Make sure the client's lower back isn't aggressively arching off the mat.
  • The client should feel their deep stomach muscles working, not their lower back squeezing.

Target Elements

  • • Alignment of ASIS and Pubic Symphysis.
  • • Transversus Abdominis engagement.

Milestones & Failures

  • → Fail: Tucking the pelvis to compensate for weak core loading.

Rule: Move the back smoothly, bone by bone.

  • Cue them to imagine a pearl necklace lifting off a table one pearl at a time.
  • Watch out for clients just hinging up from their lower back.

Target Elements

  • • Sequential, vertebra-by-vertebra movement.

Milestones & Failures

  • → Fail: Hinge points at the thoracolumbar junction. Moving as a rigid block.

Rule: Keep the sides of the waist equally long during side-work.

  • Don't let them collapse their ribs into the mat when lying on their side.
  • Keep a small "mouse house" space under their bottom waist.

Target Elements

  • • Quadratus Lumborum and Oblique isometric endurance.

Milestones & Failures

  • → Fail: Dropping into the resting shoulder or resting hip joint.

Rule: Shoulders down and wide, away from the ears.

  • When pulling straps, cue them to pull from their back/armpits, not their neck.
  • Shoulder blades should glide smoothly, not pinch together harshly.

Target Elements

  • • Serratus anterior and latissimus dorsi engagement over upper traps.

Milestones & Failures

  • → Fail: Cervical strain or aggressive shoulder elevation during loading.

Rule: Use the footbar and straps to create joint stability.

  • Clients shouldn't lock out their knees or elbows sharply.
  • Use the springs to guide the movement, don't just let the springs throw them around.

Target Elements

  • • Active muscular control against spring tension in eccentric and concentric phases.

Milestones & Failures

  • → Fail: Hyperextending joints at the end-range of motion.
